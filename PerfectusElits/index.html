Problem

Air pollution, primarily from sources like transportation, industrial processes,
and residential heating, is a major global health crisis, causing around 7 million
spremature deaths annually. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) is particularly
dangerous due to its ability to penetrate deep into the lungs and bloodstream,
leading to severe health issues such as stroke, heart disease, and lung cancer.
Addressing this problem is crucial not only for improving public health but also for
combating climate change, as both share common pollution sources.

Solution

The proposed solution is an innovative air filtration system that employs electrostatic forces generated through
friction to capture fine particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) without the need for high voltages. This system utilizes
a motor to create a continuous electrostatic charge between friction plates and a filter, effectively removing
harmful particles from the air. It includes sensors to monitor air quality and adjust the system's performance in real time, providing a cost-efficient, low-maintenance alternative to traditional HEPA filters. By improving air quality, this technology can significantly enhance public health and support environmental sustainability.
